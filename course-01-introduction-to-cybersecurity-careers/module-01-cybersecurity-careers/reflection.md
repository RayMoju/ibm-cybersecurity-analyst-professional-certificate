## My Understanding

- Cybersecurity matters because the more society relies on digital systems, the more damage cyberattacks can cause
- Businesses need cybersecurity to protect data, prevent attacks, investigate incidents, and maintain trust
- Cybersecurity is not one role; different paths focus on defence, encryption, investigation, engineering, or ethical hacking

- Cybersecurity needed because businesses are becoming more digital
- More digital systems, data, and transactions = more need for protection
- CybSec Also supports trust, compliance, privacy, and business continuity
- Field growing because threats are increasing faster than many organisations can handle

  
- Cybersecurity is not just technical work
- It is about protecting people, businesses, trust, data, and daily life
- The field suits people who like learning, investigation, problem-solving, responsibility, and helping people

- Cybersecurity careers grew from informal IT responsibilities into a major global profession
- The field keeps growing because technology keeps advancing
- As cyber threats become more advanced, organisations need more cybersecurity professionals
- This makes cybersecurity a strong field for job security and long-term learning

- Cybersecurity is not only for people with a perfect technical background,Technical knowledge matters, but mindset matters too
- Different careers can bring useful skills into cyber
- Main things needed: learning attitude, problem solving, patience, training, and networking
- To grow in cyber, I need to keep learning and stay connected to the field

- Cybersecurity specialist = broad defence and controls
- Cybercrime analyst = cybercrime patterns and evidence
- Incident and intrusion analyst = breach response and recovery
- IT auditor = compliance, risk, and assurance

- Cybersecurity analyst = defence, policies, assessments, incident planning
- Cybersecurity consultant = advice, audits, strategy, training
- Penetration/vulnerability tester = ethical testing and finding weaknesses
- Same goal: protect CIA

- Day-to-day work varies heavily by role
- Red teaming = penetration testing
- Career progression can shift from:
  - find issues
  - resolve issues
  - work with teams to ensure issues are closed

- Cyber needs strong soft skills for negotiation, communication, and cross-team work
- SIEM work can involve:
  - analysing/correlating logs
  - generating alerts
  - deciding which business/security situations actually need alerts
 
- Cybersecurity Manager = programme and team leadership
- CISO = executive strategy and risk
- Cybersecurity Engineer = builds security systems
- Cybersecurity Architect = designs overall security structure
