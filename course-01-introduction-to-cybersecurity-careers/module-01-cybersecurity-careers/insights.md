# Module 1 : Cybersecurity Careers

# The Cybersecurity Field


## Why Cybersecurity

- Cybersecurity = shield against malicious cyber threats
- Protects information, systems, digital assets, sensitive data, and transactions
- Needed across many industries
- Greater digital reliance increases the need for security
- Cyber roles can offer competitive salaries but also serious responsibility

## Roles Mentioned

- **Security analyst** — spots vulnerabilities and helps protect systems
- **Cryptographer** — protects sensitive information using encryption
- **Ethical hacker** — mimics attacker methods to strengthen defences
- **Cyber analyst** — protects sensitive data and transactions
- **Cybercrime analyst** — investigates cybercrime and malicious activity
- **Cybersecurity engineer** — helps set up and secure systems/networks
- **Information security specialist** — protects information and applies security protocols

## Cybersecurity vs Information Security

- Cybersecurity = subset of information security
- Cybersecurity protects digital systems, networks, programs, data, and assets
- Threats include hacking, malware, unauthorised access
- Controls include firewalls, encryption, intrusion detection systems
  
- Information security = broader, protects all forms of info, physical and digital
- Includes policies, procedures, access controls, employee awareness, incident response
- Focuses on CIA: confidentiality, integrity, availability

## Cybersecurity Market and Current Landscape

- Market includes cyber solutions and security services
- Growth linked to cybercrime, digital dependency, privacy concerns, stricter regulations, business risk

- Cyberattacks causing damage worth trillions
- Threats increasing in volume and complexity
- Demand growing for better security tools, services, and professionals

- Orgs trying to see and analyse more security logs to detect threats better
- AI being used to analyse large amounts of security data
- More organisations relying on third-party security providers / outsourcing
- Better security and privacy can improve customer trust


## Expert Viewpoints: The Cybersecurity Field

- Cyber work helps protect people, data, customers, and organisations
- Security mindset focuses on the safest/best approach, not always the fastest or cheapest
- Field is fast-moving and requires continuous learning
- New threats create new challenges, tools, and approaches
- Work can involve investigation, problem-solving, and responding to incidents
- Cybersecurity combines:
  - technology
  - business
  - regulation
  - psychology
  - sociology
- Cybersecurity work has pride and purpose because it protects people, Similar to firefighters/police 
- Can offer both financial reward and sense of purpose
  
## History and Growth of Cybersecurity Careers

- Cybersecurity was initially not a formal job title
- Security work often fell to general IT staff
- Early professionals relied on:
  - IT fundamentals
  - problem-solving
  - learning by doing

- Around 2005, universities began adding more cybersecurity study and dedicated degrees
- Around 2010, certifications became more important for proving knowledge
- Examples mentioned:
  - CISSP
  - CEH

- Cybersecurity workforce grew rapidly but skills shortages remained
- By 2020, almost 3 million people worked in cybersecurity
- Field was still short by around 4 million professionals worldwide
- By 2023, cybersecurity was one of the fastest-growing job categories
- Many organisations still reported unfilled cybersecurity roles
  
## How to Get Into the Cybersecurity Field

- Don’t need to come from a purely technical background
- Useful traits:
  - problem-solving attitude
  - attention to detail
  - patience
  - willingness to face challenges

## Backgrounds That Can Transfer Into Cybersecurity
- Non-technical backgrounds can bring useful skills:
  - armed forces/veterans = strategy and risk assessment
  - law enforcement = investigation and evidence gathering
  - public service = awareness of government/security processes
  - project managers = organisation and communication
  - technical writers = explaining technical info clearly

- Technical backgrounds may transition more smoothly:
  - IT support/help desk = understanding of IT infrastructure
  - software developers = coding and system knowledge
  - networking specialists = understanding networks and threats

## Training Options

- Online courses = flexible way to build foundations
- Certifications = proof/benchmark of knowledge
- Certification bodies mentioned:
  - ISC2
  - CompTIA

- Certs can help because they appear in job postings
- Bootcamps = more intensive, hands-on training
- Diplomas/degrees = deeper academic route
- Internships/apprenticeships = real-world experience + networking

## Continuous Learning

- Cybersecurity requires lifelong learning as Threats and technology keep changing
- Need to stay updated on:
  - vulnerabilities
  - countermeasures
  - best practices
  - new attacks
  - new tools

- Ways to keep learning:
  - courses
  - advanced certifications
  - webinars
  - conferences
  - seminars
  - online cyber communities

## Entry-Level Careers and Responsibilities 

- Cybersecurity has multiple entry-level paths
- Each role supports security in different ways
- Entry-level roles mentioned:
  - cybersecurity specialist
  - cybercrime analyst
  - incident and intrusion analyst
  - IT auditor
 
## Cybersecurity Specialist

- Popular entry-level cyber role
- Sets up and applies security protocols
- Protects systems and data
- Runs system audits and checks for vulnerabilities
- Manages data access policies
- Works with IT teams to protect confidentiality and integrity
- May support staff awareness training and incident response


## Cybercrime Analyst

- Investigates cyber threats and cybercrime
- Looks for patterns using past data and current trends
- Helps predict future attacks
- Supports stronger defence strategies
- May collect digital evidence and report findings

 
 ## Incident and Intrusion Analyst
 
- Handles and investigates security breaches
- Finds breach source and assesses damage
- Contains impact
- Investigates how intrusion happened
- Supports recovery and improved defences

 ## IT Auditor
- often overlooked
- Reviews IT systems, practices, and operations
- Checks compliance with laws and standards
- Finds security risks and weaknesses
- Recommends improvements
- Tests whether security measures are working

## Mid-Level Careers

- Need deeper cyber understanding and proven ability
- More responsibility and more strategic work
- Focus on secure networks, incidents, policies, and procedures
- Can be foothold for senior roles
- Important against more sophisticated threats

## Cybersecurity Analyst

- Maintains strong security structure
- Oversees security policies and protocols
- Detects, responds to, and reduces threats
- Performs vulnerability assessments and penetration testing
- Helps with incident response and recovery plans

## Cybersecurity Consultant

- External expert giving security advice
- Audits systems, procedures, and infrastructure
- Finds vulnerabilities and improvement areas
- Presents findings and solutions to senior management
- Helps design stronger cyber strategies
- May provide staff training

## Penetration and Vulnerability Tester

- Finds weaknesses before attackers do
- Mimics attacker methods ethically
- Uses automated and manual testing
- Documents vulnerabilities, exploitation process, and impact
- Recommends fixes and stronger security measures


  ## Advanced-Level Careers

- More leadership, strategy, architecture, and responsibility

## Cybersecurity Manager

- Leads cyber programme and security teams
- Develops security policies
- Manages threats, compliance, and incident response
- Works with senior management

## CISO

- Top-level security executive
- Sets organisation-wide security vision and strategy
- Manages major cyber risk
- Leads security culture and major incident response
- Works with executives, regulators, and partners

## Cybersecurity Engineer

- Designs and builds secure systems/networks
- Implements security controls
- Tests protections
- Works with IT teams to strengthen infrastructure

## Cybersecurity Architect

- Designs overall security architecture
- Defines how security components work together
- Sets security standards and direction
- Designs systems to reduce risk
