
# Module 3 : Cybersecurity Certifications

## Benefits of Cybersecurity Certifications

- Validate skills.
- Add credibility.
- Stand out to employers.
- Demonstrate expertise & commitment.
- Complement/replace a degree.
- Support promotions & career growth.
- Build interview confidence.
- Networking opportunities.
- Encourage continuous learning.

## Expert Viewpoints: Industry Certifications

- Master's/PhD can be as valuable as certifications (depends on career path).
- Consultancy roles often require certifications.
- Entry-level: Certified in Cybersecurity (CC) / CompTIA Security+.
- Long-term goal: CISSP (gold standard).
- Security+ recommended for SOC/Security Analyst roles.
- Specialise later (e.g. CISM, CEH, Cloud certs).
- Certifications are lifelong learning.

## Cybersecurity Certifications

- **ISC²** – CC (entry-level), CISSP (gold standard, 5+ years).
- **CompTIA** – Security+, CySA+, CASP+, PenTest+
- **EC-Council** – CEH (Certified Ethical Hacker).
- **GIAC** – Job role-specific certifications (e.g. DFIR, pentesting).
- **Industry-specific** – e.g. HCISPP (healthcare), finance certifications.

## CompTIA Certifications

- **A+** – Foundational IT.
- **Network+** – Networking fundamentals.
- **Security+** – Entry-level cybersecurity.
- **CySA+** – Security analytics & threat detection.
- **PenTest+** – Penetration testing & vulnerability assessment.
- **CASP+** – Advanced enterprise security & leadership.

## ISC² Certifications

- **CC** – Entry-level cybersecurity.
- **SSCP** – Systems & infrastructure security.
- **CCSP** – Cloud security.
- **CISSP** – Advanced cybersecurity (gold standard).
- **CSSLP** – Secure software development (SDLC).
- **CGRC** – IT governance, risk & compliance.

## Other Cybersecurity Certifications

### GIAC
- **GCED** – Enterprise defence.
- **GCIA** – Intrusion detection.
- **GX-FA** – Digital forensics.
- **GX-IH** – Incident response.
- **GLEG** – Cyber law & investigations.

### ISACA
- **CISA** – IT auditing.
- **CRISC** – Risk management.
- **CDPSE** – Data privacy.

### EC-Council
- **CEH** – Ethical hacking.
- **C|CISO** – Security leadership.

### Industry-Specific
- **HCISPP** – Healthcare security.
- **CERP / CRCM** – Banking risk & compliance.
- **PCIP** – Payment security.
- **GICSP / GCIP** – Industrial & critical infrastructure security.

## Expert Tips: Certification Exams

- Practice, practice, practice.
- Certification exams are challenging.
- Use multiple resources (books, videos, courses).
- Learn in the way that suits you.
- Review content multiple times.
- Focus on understanding concepts.
- Read beyond the course.

## More Key Terms
- **Black Hat Hacker** – Illegally exploits vulnerabilities for malicious purposes.
- **White Hat Hacker** – Ethically finds vulnerabilities to improve security.

## Landing Your First Cybersecurity Job

### Education
- Cybersecurity is a broad field.
- CS degree provides the strongest technical foundation.
- IT/Data Science can also transition into cyber.

### Job Search
- Network first, then apply.
- Join ISSA/ISACA and attend events.
- Talk to professors and industry professionals.
- Ask for referrals/recommendations.

### Interviews
- Know your CV in detail.
- Use projects, leadership and coursework if you lack experience.
- Be authentic.
- Talk through your reasoning.
- Ask clarifying questions.

### First 90 Days
- Learn team documentation.
- Take work that accelerates learning.
- Ask good questions (show what you've tried first).
- Build visibility by presenting your work.
- Leverage your network.

- Explore mr robot and blown to bits for perosnal curiosity

## Final Expert Career Tips 

- Show passion through personal/open-source projects.
- Be curious and think like an attacker.
- Cybersecurity requires lifelong learning.
- Combine certifications with hands-on experience.
- Find a mentor.
- Don't get discouraged, it's challenging but rewarding.
